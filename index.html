01 About

I design the part of an autonomous system that has to make a decision and commit to it — the planner. My question is always the same: how does a robot choose what to do next, in real time, when it isn't certain about the world?

I didn't start in research. I started shipping. At Flux Auto, I built real-time C++ planning and control for autonomous forklifts and tractors — global coverage planners, local trajectory generation, and multi-robot coordination that ran in production across two countries.

That production grounding is why my research reads differently from purely academic work. My M.S. thesis at the University of Minnesota applies active inference to trajectory prediction and control — a more principled way to handle uncertainty — validated in CARLA and on real perception hardware.
